# CDM 2015 - Complete Requirements Guide for Tender Proposals

## Overview

The Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015 (CDM 2015) apply to **ALL** construction projects in the UK, regardless of size or duration. Every construction tender proposal **MUST** demonstrate compliance with CDM 2015.

## Critical: What MUST Be in Every Proposal

### 1. Duty Holder Identification ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Regulatory Requirement:**
- Regulation 5 (Client duties)
- Regulation 6 (Principal Designer duties)
- Regulation 8 (Designer duties)
- Regulation 13 (Principal Contractor duties)
- Regulation 15 (Contractor duties)

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- **Named individuals or organizations** for each duty holder role (not generic statements)
- **Evidence of competence** - qualifications, training, experience for each duty holder
- **Organizational structure** - clear diagram or description showing duty holder relationships
- **Contact information** - for key duty holders
- **Alternative arrangements** - what happens if duty holders change

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "All CDM 2015 duty holders have been clearly identified for this project. The Principal Designer will be [Name/Organization] with [relevant qualifications]. The Principal Contractor will be [Name/Organization] with [relevant qualifications and certifications]."

### 2. Pre-Construction Information ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Regulatory Requirement:**
- Regulation 4 (Pre-construction information)

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- **Process for receiving and reviewing** pre-construction information
- **Information gap identification** - how you'll identify missing information
- **How information informs design** - demonstrate link between information and decisions
- **Information management systems** - how information is stored, accessed, and distributed
- **Timeline** - when information will be gathered and reviewed

**Key Elements of Pre-Construction Information:**
- Project description and timeline
- Client considerations and constraints
- Site hazards and existing conditions
- Emergency procedures and contacts
- Existing services and utilities (location, isolation points)
- Structural information
- Environmental information
- Previous surveys and reports

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "We will establish a systematic process for receiving, reviewing, and managing pre-construction information. All information will be stored in our project information management system, accessible to all relevant parties. We will conduct a gap analysis to identify any missing information and proactively work with the client to obtain necessary details."

### 3. Construction Phase Plan ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Regulatory Requirement:**
- Regulation 12 (Construction phase plan)

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- **Development process** - how and when the plan will be prepared
- **Required elements:**
  - Project description and key dates
  - Management structure and responsibilities
  - Health and safety aims
  - Site rules
  - Emergency procedures
  - Welfare facilities (location, type, maintenance)
  - Arrangements for consultation with workers
  - Security arrangements
- **Review and update procedures** - how plan will be maintained
- **Communication** - how plan will be communicated to all workers
- **Integration** - how plan integrates with existing systems

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "A comprehensive construction phase plan will be developed before any work commences. The plan will include detailed arrangements for health and safety management, site rules, emergency procedures, welfare facilities, and worker consultation. The plan will be reviewed regularly and updated as the project progresses. All workers will receive site-specific inductions based on the construction phase plan."

### 4. Health and Safety File ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Regulatory Requirement:**
- Regulation 12 (Health and safety file)

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- **Compilation process** - how file will be maintained throughout project
- **Required contents:**
  - Brief description of the work carried out
  - Any residual hazards and how they should be managed
  - Key structural principles and unusual or hazardous materials used
  - Information regarding removal or dismantling of installed plant and equipment
  - Health and safety information about equipment provided
  - Location and nature of services, including emergency isolation points
- **Management responsibility** - who is responsible for maintaining file
- **Handover process** - how and when file will be delivered to client
- **Format and accessibility** - digital/paper, how it can be accessed

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "We will maintain a comprehensive health and safety file throughout the project lifecycle. The file will be compiled progressively as work proceeds and will contain all information necessary for future construction work, maintenance, cleaning, and demolition. On project completion, the file will be handed over to the client in both digital and hard copy formats."

### 5. Worker Welfare Facilities ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Regulatory Requirement:**
- Regulation 13(4) and Schedule 2

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- **Sanitary facilities:**
  - Toilets (clean, ventilated, adequately lit, separate for men/women or lockable)
  - Washing facilities (hot and cold water, soap, towels, near toilets)
- **Rest facilities:**
  - Rest rooms for breaks and meals
  - Protection from adverse weather
  - Seating and tables
  - Means of heating food and boiling water
- **Drinking water** - readily accessible, clearly marked
- **Changing facilities** - where special clothing is required
- **Storage facilities** - for clothing and personal protective equipment
- **Location and accessibility** - near work areas, accessible to all
- **Maintenance** - regular cleaning and maintenance arrangements

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "Welfare facilities will be provided in accordance with CDM 2015 Schedule 2. This includes sanitary facilities, rest facilities, drinking water, and storage facilities. All facilities will be located conveniently near work areas, maintained to high standards, and accessible to all workers."

### 6. Competence and Training ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Regulatory Requirement:**
- Regulation 8 (Competence of persons)

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- **Competency verification process** - how you verify competence before work starts
- **Required qualifications** - what qualifications are needed for each role
- **Training arrangements:**
  - Site-specific inductions for all workers
  - Ongoing training and development
  - Toolbox talks and briefings
- **Monitoring** - how you monitor and assess ongoing competence
- **Contractor verification** - how you verify competence of subcontractors

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "All persons working on this project will be competent for the work they undertake. We operate a rigorous competency verification process, checking qualifications, training, and experience before work commences. All workers receive site-specific inductions, and ongoing training is provided as needed. Subcontractor competence is verified through pre-qualification and ongoing monitoring."

### 7. Risk Management ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Regulatory Requirement:**
- Regulation 8(3) - Designers must eliminate or reduce risks
- Regulation 9(1) - Principal Designer must ensure designers comply

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- **Design risk assessment:**
  - Process for identifying risks at design stage
  - Hierarchy of control: eliminate, reduce, control, inform
  - How risks inform design decisions
- **Construction risk assessment:**
  - Risk assessments and method statements (RAMS)
  - Identification of significant risks
  - Control measures for each risk
- **Risk register** - central register of all identified risks
- **Review process** - regular review and update of risk assessments
- **Communication** - how risks are communicated to relevant parties

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "Risk management is embedded throughout our design and construction processes. Designers apply the hierarchy of risk control to eliminate or reduce risks at source. Detailed risk assessments and method statements are prepared for all construction activities. A central risk register is maintained and regularly reviewed. All significant risks are communicated to relevant parties."

### 8. Coordination and Cooperation ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Regulatory Requirement:**
- Regulation 11 - Principal Designer coordination
- Regulation 13(5) - Principal Contractor coordination

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- **Principal Designer and Principal Contractor liaison:**
  - Regular coordination meetings
  - Information sharing protocols
  - Communication channels and frequency
- **Multi-contractor coordination:**
  - How contractors will work together on site
  - Coordination meetings and briefings
  - Conflict resolution procedures
- **Integration of activities:**
  - How design and construction activities are integrated
  - How different trades coordinate

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "Effective coordination and cooperation between all duty holders is fundamental to our approach. The Principal Designer and Principal Contractor will maintain regular liaison, with formal coordination meetings at key project milestones. All contractors will be integrated into our coordination processes through regular site briefings and meetings."

## Project Types

### Notifiable Projects

Projects are **notifiable** if:
- Construction phase > 30 days, OR
- Construction phase > 500 person days, OR
- Involves specific high-risk activities

**Additional Requirements for Notifiable Projects:**
- Principal Designer **must** be appointed
- Principal Contractor **must** be appointed
- HSE notification (F10 form) must be submitted
- More detailed pre-construction information required
- Enhanced construction phase plan requirements

### Small Projects

Small projects are still subject to CDM 2015, but a **proportionate approach** is acceptable.

**Still Required:**
- Duty holder identification
- Basic construction phase plan
- Appropriate welfare facilities
- Worker competence verification
- Health and safety file (even for small projects)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid ‚ùå

1. **Assuming CDM 2015 doesn't apply** - It applies to ALL projects
2. **Vague duty holder identification** - Name specific individuals/organizations
3. **Focusing only on construction phase** - Address all phases (pre-construction, construction, handover)
4. **Generic risk statements** - Provide project-specific risk assessments
5. **No mention of health and safety file** - It's mandatory for all projects
6. **Insufficient welfare facility details** - Be specific about location, type, maintenance

## Winning Strategies üèÜ

1. **Visual Aids**: Include organograms showing duty holder relationships
2. **Case Studies**: Provide examples of previous CDM 2015 compliant projects
3. **Third-Party Verification**: Mention audits, certifications, external validations
4. **Continuous Improvement**: Show lessons learned and improvement processes
5. **Innovation**: Demonstrate use of technology for CDM compliance
6. **Integration**: Show how CDM requirements are integrated throughout proposal
7. **Detail**: Provide specific, detailed responses rather than generic statements
8. **Evidence**: Support claims with qualifications, certifications, experience

## Evaluation Criteria

Evaluators will assess:

- **Understanding**: Do you understand CDM 2015 requirements?
- **Practical Application**: Can you apply CDM 2015 in practice?
- **Competence**: Are your people and organization competent?
- **Comprehensiveness**: Have you addressed all requirements?

CDM 2015 compliance is **not optional** - it's mandatory for all construction projects. Your proposal must demonstrate:

- Practical application of requirements
- Evidence of competence
- Comprehensive coverage of all requirements

Use this guide to ensure your proposal addresses every CDM 2015 requirement comprehensively.
