# Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 - Complete Requirements Guide

## Overview

The Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 (HSWA 1974) is the primary piece of UK health and safety legislation. It applies to **ALL** employers and employees in the UK, including all construction projects. Every construction tender proposal **MUST** demonstrate compliance with HSWA 1974.

## Critical: What MUST Be in Every Proposal

Proposals must:
- **Explicitly state compliance** with HSWA 1974
- **Identify our role** (Principal Contractor, Contractor, Employer, etc.)
- **Demonstrate understanding** of our duties
- **Outline how we will fulfill** each requirement
- **Reference specific section numbers** (e.g., "In accordance with Section 2(2)(a)...")

## SECTION 2: General Duties of Employers to Employees

### Section 2(1) - General Duty
"It shall be the duty of every employer to ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, the health, safety and welfare at work of all his employees."

### Section 2(2) - Specific Duties

#### Section 2(2)(a) - Safe Systems of Work ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Requirement:** Provide and maintain safe systems of work

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Demonstrate safe system of work development and implementation
- Show process for identifying, evaluating and controlling workplace hazards
- Detail how safe systems of work are documented and communicated
- Explain how systems are reviewed and updated
- Provide examples of safe systems of work in practice

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "In accordance with Section 2(2)(a) of HSWA 1974, we will provide and maintain safe systems of work for all employees. Our safe systems of work are developed through comprehensive risk assessment and consultation with employees, documented in method statements, and communicated through inductions and training."

#### Section 2(2)(b) - Safe Plant and Substances ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Requirement:** Make arrangements for ensuring safety in connection with the use, handling, storage and transport of articles and substances

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Show plant and equipment selection and maintenance procedures
- Demonstrate substance handling and storage arrangements (COSHH compliance)
- Detail equipment inspection and maintenance schedules
- Explain hazardous substance management
- Provide evidence of competent plant operators

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "Under Section 2(2)(b), we will ensure all plant and equipment is safe and suitable for use. All substances are handled, stored and transported safely in accordance with COSHH regulations. Regular inspections and maintenance ensure plant remains safe throughout its use."

#### Section 2(2)(c) - Information, Instruction, Training and Supervision ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Requirement:** Provide such information, instruction, training and supervision as is necessary to ensure health and safety at work

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Detail information, instruction, training and supervision arrangements
- Show training needs analysis and competency frameworks
- Demonstrate supervision structures and ratios
- Explain ongoing training and refresher programs
- Provide evidence of qualified trainers and supervisors

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "In accordance with Section 2(2)(c), we will provide adequate information, instruction, training and supervision to all employees. All employees receive job-specific training before starting work, and ongoing supervision ensures continued safe working practices."

#### Section 2(2)(d) - Safe Workplace with Safe Access and Egress ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Requirement:** Maintain any place of work under their control in a condition that is safe and without risks to health

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Demonstrate workplace inspection and maintenance procedures
- Show safe access and egress arrangements
- Detail emergency evacuation procedures and routes
- Explain workplace housekeeping standards
- Provide site layout and access control measures

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "Under Section 2(2)(d), we will maintain safe workplaces with safe access and egress. Regular workplace inspections ensure hazards are identified and controlled. Safe access and egress routes are clearly marked and maintained, and emergency evacuation routes are planned, tested and communicated to all employees."

#### Section 2(2)(e) - Safe Working Environment and Welfare Facilities ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Requirement:** Provide and maintain a working environment that is safe, without risks to health, and adequate as regards facilities and arrangements for welfare at work

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Detail welfare facility provisions (toilets, washing, rest facilities)
- Show environmental controls (temperature, ventilation, lighting)
- Demonstrate first aid arrangements and facilities
- Explain arrangements for protecting employees from workplace hazards
- Provide welfare facility maintenance and cleaning procedures

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "In accordance with Section 2(2)(e), we will provide and maintain a safe working environment with adequate welfare facilities. Welfare facilities comply with Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992. Environmental conditions are maintained within acceptable limits, and adequate first aid facilities and trained first aiders are provided."

#### Section 2(6) - Consultation with Employees ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Requirement:** Consult with safety representatives or employees about health and safety matters

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Show employee consultation arrangements
- Detail safety representative arrangements (if applicable)
- Demonstrate consultation on health and safety policies and procedures
- Explain how employee concerns are addressed
- Provide examples of consultation in practice

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "Under Section 2(6), we will consult with employees on health and safety matters. Employee consultation is integral to our health and safety management approach. Safety representatives are consulted on significant health and safety matters, and employee suggestions and concerns are actively sought and addressed."

## SECTION 3: General Duties to Persons Other Than Employees

### Section 3(1) - Ensure Work Does Not Endanger Public ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Requirement:** Conduct undertaking so that persons not in employment (including public, neighbours, visitors, other contractors) are not exposed to risks to their health or safety

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Demonstrate public safety measures
- Show site security and access control
- Detail public protection measures (barriers, hoardings, signage)
- Explain traffic and pedestrian management
- Provide emergency procedures affecting public

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "In accordance with Section 3(1) of HSWA 1974, we will conduct our work so as not to endanger the public. Site security and access control prevent unauthorised public access. Public areas adjacent to our work are protected by appropriate barriers and signage, and traffic and pedestrian management protects the public from construction activities."

### Section 3(2) - Provide Information About Risks ‚úÖ MANDATORY

**Requirement:** Provide information about risks and how they are controlled to persons who may be affected

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Show process for identifying persons who may be affected by work
- Detail information sharing with neighbours and public
- Demonstrate pre-work communication and consultation
- Explain ongoing communication during work
- Provide examples of public information and consultation

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "Under Section 3(2), we will provide information about risks to persons who may be affected by our work. Neighbours and members of the public are informed about work activities and associated risks before work commences, and ongoing communication ensures affected parties are kept informed."

## SECTIONS 7-8: General Duties of Employees

### Section 7(a) - Take Reasonable Care ‚úÖ MANDATORY TO COMMUNICATE

**Requirement:** Every employee must take reasonable care for the health and safety of themselves and others

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Demonstrate how employee duties are communicated
- Show training on employee responsibilities
- Detail expectations for employee behaviour
- Explain disciplinary procedures for non-compliance
- Provide evidence of employee awareness of duties

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "All employees are informed of their duty to take reasonable care for health and safety under Section 7(a) of HSWA 1974. Employee responsibilities are communicated through inductions and training, and we expect all employees to take reasonable care for their own safety and that of others."

### Section 7(b) - Cooperation with Employer ‚úÖ MANDATORY TO COMMUNICATE

**Requirement:** Employees must cooperate with their employer in connection with health and safety duties

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Show how cooperation is expected and achieved
- Detail employee participation in health and safety
- Demonstrate consultation and involvement mechanisms
- Explain how employee cooperation is encouraged
- Provide examples of successful cooperation

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "We expect all employees to cooperate with health and safety requirements under Section 7(b). Employee cooperation is essential to our health and safety management, and we actively encourage and facilitate employee participation in health and safety."

### Section 8 - Not Interfere with Safety Measures ‚úÖ MANDATORY TO COMMUNICATE

**Requirement:** No person shall intentionally or recklessly interfere with or misuse anything provided in the interests of health, safety or welfare

**What to Include in Proposal:**
- Show policies on interference with safety measures
- Detail consequences for misuse of safety equipment
- Demonstrate monitoring and enforcement
- Explain equipment maintenance and replacement procedures
- Provide evidence of equipment inspection and control

**Example Proposal Language:**
> "All persons are informed that interference with safety measures is prohibited under Section 8 of HSWA 1974. Safety equipment and measures are protected from misuse or interference, and disciplinary action will be taken against anyone who interferes with or misuses safety equipment."

## Key Legal Terms

### "So Far As Is Reasonably Practicable"

This is the standard applied throughout HSWA 1974. It means:
- **Duty is not absolute** - balance of risk against cost/time/trouble
- **If risk is high**, cost is less relevant
- **If risk is low**, cost may be considered
- **Must be justifiable** - not just convenient

**Use in Proposals:**
- State "so far as is reasonably practicable" when referencing duties
- Show how risk assessments inform "reasonably practicable" decisions
- Demonstrate that health and safety is not compromised for cost

## Integration with CDM 2015

HSWA 1974 and CDM 2015 work together:
- **HSWA 1974** = Primary legislation (general duties)
- **CDM 2015** = Specific regulations for construction projects

**Both must be addressed in proposals:**
- CDM 2015 provides specific construction requirements
- HSWA 1974 provides underlying general duties
- Together they ensure comprehensive health and safety management

**In Proposals:**
- Address HSWA 1974 general duties
- Address CDM 2015 specific construction requirements
- Show how they integrate in your approach
- Reference both in relevant sections

## Common Mistakes to Avoid ‚ùå

1. **Not mentioning HSWA 1974** - It's mandatory for all employers
2. **Vague compliance statements** - Must reference specific sections
3. **Missing Section 3** - Duties to public are critical in construction
4. **Not explaining employee duties** - Sections 7-8 must be communicated
5. **Ignoring "reasonably practicable"** - Key legal concept that must be understood
6. **Separating HSWA and CDM** - They work together, show integration
